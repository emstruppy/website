Children's Museum Exhibit

I participated in a junior level design class to design a STEM exhibit for a local children's museum. My groups project explored the concept of elastic and inelastic collisions by the use of cars on a track. I acted as lead design and 3D modeled the tracks and magnet holder pieces on the fronts of the cars in SolidWorks. I also got trained in the woodshop to be able to cut and sand the wooden parts. The cars could be turned so that if pushed together they would attract and if the magnets were opposing they would repel.

Subsea 7 Pipeline Design Internship

This past summer I interned at Subsea 7 in Houston,TX as a Pipeline Design Intern. My task was to compare mitigation methods for lateral buckling of high pressure high temperature subsea oil and gas pipelines. To do my comparison I used a company developed tool to create a finite element model of the specific pipeline under different cases using sleepers as mitigation. I compared compressive longitudinal strain and amplitude with the actual pipeline that utilizes rotating buoyancy modules. I also wrote a technical note and presented to all the department managers.
